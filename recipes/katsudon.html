Katsudon

Description

Katsudon is a popular Japanese dish. It is a deep-fried pork cutlet that has been cooked with onions, a soy-sauce based sauce and lightly cooked eggs and then served on top of rice.

Ingredients

  • fried breaded pork chop
  • 1/2 cup dashi stock
  • sugar
  • water
  • soy sauce
  • mirin
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 onion
  • steamed white rice
  • scallion

Steps

  1. Place frying pan on stove over medium heat and pour in enough water to fill the bottom of the frying pan.
  2. Add in the dashi stock, sugar, soy sauce and mirin.
  3. After that, place the onion into the mixture and cover the pan with a lid.
  4. While the onion is being cooked, lightly beat 2 eggs.
  5. If you haven't already, slice the katsu into strips.
  6. Once the onion has started to soften, place the katsu on top of the onions and then drizzle the egg over everything.
  7. Place the lid back onto the pan and cook over medium low heat until the egg is just set.
  8. Garnish with sliced scallions and server over a bowl of steamed rice.
