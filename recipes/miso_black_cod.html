Miso Black Cod

Description

Black cod with miso is a classic Japanese seafood dish that is served at many formal Japnese restaurants. It is made by marinating the fish in a sake-miso marinade resulting in a delicate and buttery dish.

Ingredients

  • white miso paste
  • water
  • mirin
  • sake
  • brown sugar
  • salt
  • black cod fillets

Steps

  1. Sprinkle salt over the fish and set it aside for 30 minutes.
  2. Whisk the miso, water, mirin, sake and brown sugar together in a small skillet over medium heat until mixture simmers and thickens slightly.
  3. Transfer the marinade into a flat-bottomed airtight container and let it cool.
  4. Pour 2 tbsp of sake over the fish to rinse off the salt and gently pat dry with a paper towel to remove the moisture.
  5. Once the marinade has cooled, place the fish in the marinade and coat the fish on both sides.
  6. Cover the container and keep it in the refrigerator. You can let it marinade for anywhere between 1 to 3 days depending on how salty you want the dish.
  7. After letting it marinade, take the fish out of the container and scrape off all the marinade completely.
  8. Preheat oven to 200 degrees. Place parchment paper on a baking pan and oil it.
  9. Bake the fish fish until the surface is browned a bit. This takes around 20 minutes.
  10. Serve with rice.
